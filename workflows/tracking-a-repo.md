# Tracking a Repo

## Scenario

Your team adopted a new service, or you need to contribute to a repo you have not touched before. You need to work on it alongside everything else you have.

---

## Without the Town

You clone the repo. You pick a location on disk. Maybe it goes next to your other repos if you have a convention for that. Maybe it goes in a project directory for the thing you are building. You make a mental note, or an actual note, about where you put it.

There is no connection between this repo and your others. No shared conventions about where working areas live. No shared awareness. If your AI assistant knows about your other repos, it does not know this one exists. You will have to tell it.

Later, when you need to come back to this repo, you will look for it. You will check the places you usually put things. It might take a moment. It might take longer.

---

## With the Town

You point the town at the repo URL. One operation.

The repo lands in the standard location your workspace defines. Your working conventions apply automatically. Your AI assistant can discover it the same way it discovers every other repo you work with.

When you need to come back to it, you navigate the same way you navigate everything else.

---

## Concepts Involved

**[Workspace](../concepts/workspace.md)**: Tracked repos live in a defined structure, not wherever you happened to put them. The workspace is what makes "where is that repo" a non-question.

**[AI Conventions](../concepts/ai-conventions.md)**: Because the repo lives in the workspace's standard structure, your AI assistant can discover it automatically. You do not have to reintroduce your codebase each time you switch what you are working on.
